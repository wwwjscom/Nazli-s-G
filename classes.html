Introduction to Information Retrieval

Prerequisite: COCS 173 and Strong Programming knowledge.
Overview of fundamental issues of information retrieval with theoretical foundations. The Information-retrieval techniques and theory, covering both effectiveness and run-time performance of information-retrieval systems are covered. The focus is on algorithms and heuristics used to find documents relevant to the user request and to find them fast. The course covers the architecture and components of the search engine such as parser, stemmer, index builder, and query processor. The students learn the material by building a prototype of such a search engine. (3-0-3)

Data Mining

Prerequisite: COCS 173 and Strong Programming knowledge.
The course covers both supervised and unsupervised algorithms. Among these are classification algorithms such as Naive Bayes, Decision Trees, Neural Networks, Support Vector Machine, Lazy learners, Rule-based classifiers. Association rule mining and clustering will be covered. Various issues in the pre-processing of the data are addressed. The students learn the material by building various data mining models and perform experimentations and provide analysis of the results.
